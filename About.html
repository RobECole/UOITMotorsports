About

In response to the formula challenge posted by the Society of Automotive Engineers, a group of dedicated students from the University of Ontario Institute of Technology have formed UOIT Motorsports. The team’s purpose is to design, budget and build their formula- style race car to compete at various international FSAE challenges.

Our team's goal is to design, fabricate and race a competitive FSAE vehicle in a multitude of Formula SAE events. The team operates on the premise that we have been contracted to produce a prototype car for evaluation and for possible production. What is FSAE?

In 1979, the Society of Automotive Engineers created the “Mini-Indy”. The event was designed to give aspiring engineers a project that would involve the design of a race car, the construction process and the project management responsibilities associated with it. By 1997, this event was renamed to “formula SAE”, and had grown to an international level. FSAE has afforded passionate engineering students an amazing opportunity in designing, constructing and managing the production of a high speed race car. These prototypes are judged based on cost, effective use of materials, and overall design concepts.
